When airlines don't have parachutes, why should P2P lending platforms have LPF?

Updated: Jul 18, 2017, 10:58 am IST

We are weeks away from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) coming out with its rules to regulate the peer to peer (P2P) lending industry in the country. A welcome step, this is something that most organized players in the sector, including us, have been pushing for.

With the regulations in place, P2P can formally be counted as one of the institutional sources of raising funds and also an investment. The gray areas are expected to be ironed out and it is widely believed that the regulations will be progressive in nature. P2P platforms are expected to be categorized as an NBFC, which is in sync with the industry expectations. The regulations will lay out the corporate structure that each of the platforms would need to follow and most importantly the DOS and Don’ts related to dealing with lenders and borrowers. However, of late, there has been an interesting trend of platforms coming up with a lender protection fund. What does it do? In case a lender loses the money he has extended to a borrower as a loan, the lender protection fund is expected to cover the losses for the investor.  On the face of it, it sounds like a good idea, but if you dig deeper, there are several issues.

Let me start with the analogy of airplanes. Airplanes do not offer people flying with them parachutes because the whole premise is that the plane should be built in a manner that its crash proof. The flyer is aware of the risk, but he trusts the plane. You have a life vest under your seat for an emergency landing on water, but you do not have an escape pod that can be activated if a flight is about to crash. Similarly, the lender on a P2P site should be able to trust that the lending platform has built a system that can help Lender earn higher returns by mitigating risk. While a P2P platform cannot shirk its responsibilities when it comes to investor protection, having a fund to mitigate losses is not the answer. Proper systemic safeguards and strong ethics should alone suffice.

At this juncture, it is important to understand what a P2P platform is for someone looking to lend money. P2P platform is an alternative source of “investment”, which means there are risks associated with it. Being an investor is similar to investing in a stock market, where you have risks, but you still invest because of better returns associated with it. Does stock market protect investor’s fund or guarantee assured returns? P2P Lending is a risk-based asset class. It has to deliver on the promise of higher returns and lower cost.

It is also important to understand that any kind of fund that looks compensate a lender would need money in it. The money has to come from somewhere, which means someone would, sooner or later, end up paying for it. The choice is the investor takes a hit on his returns so that the fund can be put in place, or the borrower is charged a higher interest rate because the cost of doing business has gone up. So LPF dilutes the very premise of the P2P lending model and is not viable in the long run.

We have been in business for some time now and as we have grown, our systems and processes have become more robust. We have inked partnerships, developed capabilities in-house and used cutting edge technology to come up with robust lending platform. Most in the sector has done the same and it is great that unlike many other countries we have not had companies cutting corners. P2P growth in the country has been steady and for most in the industry growing at all cost is not an option.

Launching LPF would in some ways signal that a platform does not have confidence in its own credit evaluation and risk-mitigation system. The lender does not invest in P2P lending to protect his principal and every platform makes it abundantly clear that P2P lending has risks associated with it. If a lender’s objective was to protect his investment, the lender would have looked at fixed deposits or investment opportunities with guaranteed returns. A lender comes to a P2P platform knowing well about the risks and rewards. It is the promise of higher returns on his investment that draws him to a P2P platform and not the protection of an investor fund. LPF dilutes returns, it gives an impression of safety to the lender, so in the end it prevents the platform from delivering on the promise they have made to their Lender.

As we await the final regulations to come into force, it would be prudent to get our systems and processes better. Instead of parking funds in LPF, a smart platform should invest in creating technology and systems that strengthen their borrower verification, credit evaluation and risk mitigation. It should focus on product innovation and offer newer opportunities to lenders to help them diversify their portfolio. The focus should remain on higher returns at lower risks. For a nascent industry a protection fund is profligacy, a distraction that can in fact lead to people being careless.
